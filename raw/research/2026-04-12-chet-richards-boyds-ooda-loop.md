# Chet Richards — "Boyd's OODA Loop" — Full Extraction

Source: Necesse 2020, Vol 5, Issue 1. Sjøkrigsskolen (Norwegian War College). Peer reviewed.

## 1. The Detailed Multi-Path Feedback Diagram (Figure 2)

Boyd drew exactly ONE OODA loop sketch in his entire body of work — in "The Essence of Winning and Losing" (1996, p. 3). Richards: "the only OODA 'loop' that Boyd actually drew." It bears almost no resemblance to the popular O→O→D→A circle.

### Full Architecture

**Four labeled columns:** Observe | Orient | Decide | Act

**OBSERVE node:**
- Inputs: "Unfolding Circumstances," "Outside Information," "Unfolding Interaction With Environment"
- Connects rightward via "Feed Forward" into Orient

**ORIENT block — the central hub (bounded multi-component box):**
Five factors interact in a web/star pattern (NOT linear):
- Cultural Traditions
- Genetic Heritage
- Analyses & Synthesis
- New Information
- Previous Experience

Bidirectional arrows among all five. Represents the actor's mental model of reality.

**From ORIENT, FOUR distinct outbound paths:**

1. **Feed Forward → Decide:** Processed understanding to Decision (Hypothesis) node
2. **Implicit Guidance & Control → Observe (top path):** Orient back LEFTWARD to Observations, bypassing Decide and Act. "Orientation shapes what we observe" feedback — the incestuous amplification path.
3. **Implicit Guidance & Control → Act (key bypass):** Orient DIRECTLY to Action, skipping Decide entirely. The fast-action path.
4. **Feedback from Act/Environment → Observe:** Two feedback arrows from right to left — from Act back to Orient (short), and from Environment back to Observe (long).

**DECIDE node:** Labeled "Decision (Hypothesis)" — NOT just Decision. Decisions are testable hypotheses.

**ACT node:** Labeled "Action (Test)" — NOT just Action. Actions are experiments.

### All Feedback Paths (Popular Version Omits These)

| Path | Direction | What It Represents |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Orient → Observe (IG&C) | Right to Left | Orientation shapes/filters observation (confirmation bias or calibrated attention) |
| Orient → Act (IG&C bypass) | Skips Decide | Implicit, intuitive action from compiled orientation — no explicit decision |
| Act/Environment → Observe (long) | Right to Left | Action results re-enter as observations |
| Act → Orient (short) | Right to Left | Action outcomes update orientation directly |
| Internal Orient web | Multidirectional | Five factors continuously reshape each other |

The popular version shows 4 arrows. Boyd's actual diagram has 8-10+, and two of the most important go in the "wrong" direction or bypass stages entirely.

## 2. Implicit Guidance & Control (IG&C) — Fast Action Without Explicit Decision

The IG&C link runs directly from Orient to Act, bypassing Decide.

> "Most of the time our actions should flow from orientation directly and implicitly, that is, without explicit (e.g., written or detailed verbal) commands or instructions."

**Mechanism:** Orientation is a compiled, running mental model — a "repertoire." When orientation is accurate and matches the situation, it triggers action without deliberation. Boyd used **Fingerspitzengefühl** ("fingertip feeling") — sensing what is needed without reasoning through it.

> "Decisions — the selection of actions — are made within Orientation."

In fast-cycle operation, the decision was already compiled into orientation during training, reflection, and experience.

**Condition:** IG&C only works when orientation is accurate AND shared. Wrong orientation + IG&C = fast, confident, wrong actions.

## 3. Orient as the Central Hub

Richards: "To get a handle on [the diagram], begin with the centrality of orientation."

**Orient filters Observe:** IG&C feedback from Orient back to Observations means orientation shapes observation. "You see what your mental model predicts."

**Incestuous amplification:** Orientation influences observation to confirm itself → strengthens orientation → narrows observation. Dangerous self-reinforcing loop. Organizations in crisis are especially vulnerable.

**Orient generates action:** Via IG&C bypass, Orient is the actual source of action in fast-cycle operations — not Decide. Decide is what you use when orientation fails to resolve automatically.

**Orient contains the mental model of reality:** The five internal factors interact in a web. "Analyses and synthesis" specifically "represents that we pull concepts from a variety of domains to create ideas for new actions."

## 4. Auftragstaktik — Shared Orientation Enabling Distributed Action

Boyd's challenge: individuals have orientations, but organizations must act. Divergent orientations → no common implicit orientation → paralysis.

**Solution: "common outlook"** — mutual trust, cohesion, unity. Boyd: "Without a common outlook superiors cannot give subordinates freedom-of-action and maintain coherency of ongoing action."

**Auftragstaktik** (mission-type orders): formal leadership philosophy of organizations as diverse as U.S. Marine Corps and Royal Norwegian Navy. Extreme case: Marquet (2012) successfully commanded nuclear submarine "without issuing virtually any explicit commands." 134 men with aligned orientation = 135 aligned actors.

**Building shared orientation:**
> "Arrange the setting and circumstances so that leaders and subordinates alike are given the opportunity to continuously interact with the external world, and with each other, in order to more quickly make many-sided implicit cross-referencing projections, empathies, correlations, and rejections."

**Boyd's organizational climate formula:** Fingerspitzengefühl + Auftragstaktik + focus-and-direction + similar implicit orientation/mutual trust

**Behindigkeit** (added late in life): The ability to break out of deeply held patterns — knowing when to abandon doctrine. The meta-capability.

## 5. Full OODA vs. Simplified Popular Version

Richards: "Boyd never included the OODA 'loop' as described by Storr and depicted in Figure 1 into any of his works, nor did he ever describe it as a sequential process."

**The Storr objection:** "Military forces do not in practice wait to observe until they have acted. Observation, orientation and action are continuous processes, and decisions are made occasionally in consequences of them. There is no OODA loop."

**Speed fallacy:** Popular version implies faster = winning. Boyd's distinction: being *inside their loop* is the goal, not faster through yours. Honda introduced 113 motorcycle models in 18 months — the decisive factor was that each pass improved orientation, not that Honda was fast.

> "Just going through some loop without learning anything is a waste of time, money, and energy, and going faster just wastes more, more quickly."

**Sequential trap:** Popular version implies you can't act until you've decided. Boyd's diagram shows Observe, Orient, Act are continuous, simultaneous processes running in parallel.

**Decision-as-bottleneck:** In circular model, every action passes through Decide. In Boyd's actual diagram, most actions bypass Decide via IG&C. This is the dominant operating mode for well-trained organizations, not an edge case.

## 6. OODA Applied to Organizations

### The Alignment Problem

> "In Boyd's scheme, only individuals have orientations."

If individual orientations diverge enough, no common orientation forms → organization cannot use IG&C → under pressure, "shatters into bickering sub-groups, takes no effective action, and perishes."

### Two Diseases of Orientation

**Incestuous amplification:** IG&C feedback from Orientation → Observation causes organizations to only see confirming data. Self-reinforcing loop increasingly disconnected from reality. Fix: external reference points (assessing from inside increases confusion).

**Confirmation cascade:** Opposing views marginalized. Data environment becomes closed loop confirming leadership beliefs.

### The Learning Loop (Figure 5)

Sub-loop embedded within OODA: Observation → Analyses & Synthesis → Hypothesis → Test → (feedback to Observation and Orient). This is the process for generating new repertoire. "Also updates our orientations, including our concepts for employing that novelty." Essentially Deming's PDCA / Toyota scientific thinking / classic scientific method embedded within OODA.

### The Dual-Circuit Model (Figure 6)

Two simultaneously active circuits:
1. **Fast IG&C path:** Orient → Act — executing current repertoire
2. **Learning loop:** Generating new repertoire, updating orientation

"Both are active, although the emphasis is on one or the other." Active engagement → IG&C emphasis. Between engagements → learning loop emphasis. Caption: "Both processes operate in harmony."

### Building Organizational Orientation (Four Mechanisms)

1. Establish a school / doctrine institution (shared foundational mental models)
2. HR as culture keeper (cultural transmission)
3. Living doctrine manual — not commandments but current best orientation, subject to kaizen
4. After-action reviews (AARs) treated with seriousness that transcends rank
