# CHI 2024 — "Generating Personas Using LLMs and Assessing Their Viability"

**Citation:** Schuller et al. (2024). Generating personas using LLMs and assessing their viability. CHI EA '24, Article 179, 7 pages. ACM.

**Publication type:** CHI Extended Abstract (7-page late-breaking work, NOT full CHI paper). This matters for confidence calibration.

**Access:** Paywalled (ACM DL). No preprint found on arXiv, ResearchGate, or OSF.

## 1. Study Methodology

Practitioner-grounded approach from real client engagement. Domain: industrial supplier of aesthetic/functional door and window fittings. Personas needed for innovation workshop. Target stakeholders: architects, investors/fabricators, end-users.

Structured prompting strategy for ChatGPT using one-shot/few-shot training — example personas embedded in prompt to calibrate tone, format, depth. Prompts incorporated stakeholder type descriptions, product feature context, expected persona output format.

Generated personas compared against hand-crafted personas by human UX researchers on the same brief. Matched set: LLM-generated vs. human-authored, same topic, same stakeholders, same format.

## 2. Blind Evaluation Protocol

- **Evaluators:** 11 UX experts and designers (domain-relevant, not crowdworkers)
- **Personas assessed:** 16 per participant
- **Blinding:** Evaluators not told which were LLM-generated vs. human-authored
- **Platform:** Remote, online (LimeSurvey)
- **Design:** Modeled on Turing test settings — discrimination task ("is this AI or human?") + quality assessment
- **Language confound:** Original human personas in German, translated to English for study. LLM personas generated directly in English.

## 3. Key Findings

**Core finding:** Personas generated by ChatGPT were "challenging to distinguish" from human-created ones and judged similarly, indicating viability with domain experts.

**Critical nuances:**
- Language is "challenging to distinguish" — NOT "statistically identical" or "perfectly indistinguishable." Hedged.
- **Translation confound:** Human personas translated from German → English. LLM personas generated natively in English. Evaluators cited language flow as key discriminating signal — this may have disadvantaged human personas on the exact criterion evaluators found most diagnostic.
- **Small sample:** 11 evaluators. Authors explicitly call for larger samples.
- **Single domain:** B2B industrial (door/window fittings). Generalizability unvalidated.
- **No diversity assessment:** Cannot assess whether LLM personas represent actual user diversity.

## 4. LLM and Prompting

- **Model:** ChatGPT (likely GPT-3.5-turbo or GPT-4, early 2024; exact version not specified)
- **Strategy:** One-shot/few-shot — example personas in prompt to shape format and style
- **Contents:** Stakeholder type, product features, output format
- **Philosophy:** "Strategic" prompting tailored to specific workshop use case, not generic templates

## 5. Persona Attributes

**Included:** Demographics (role, age, supply chain context), goals/objectives, contextual use patterns, narrative prose descriptions.

**Evaluated on:** Perceived authenticity, perceived quality, authorship discrimination, language naturalness.

**NOT explicitly reported:** Pain points as distinct attribute, emotional/empathy dimensions as scored, goals vs. frustrations breakdown, quantitative rubric per attribute.

## 6. Limitations (Author-Identified)

1. **Translation confound** — may bias comparison toward LLM
2. **Small sample** (n=11) — insufficient for robust statistics
3. **Single narrow domain** — B2B industrial only
4. **Limited stakeholders** — one industry subset
5. **No diversity validation** — cannot assess real-world representativeness
6. **Bias/stereotype risk** — LLM personas may perpetuate at scale
7. **CHI EA format** — compressed methodology (7 pages, not full paper)

## 7. Broader Literature Context

### Systematic Review of 52 GenAI Persona Papers (arXiv 2504.04927, 2025)

- **44% of papers don't clearly describe how AI personas were evaluated** — field-wide gap
- Schuller's blind discrimination test is one of few — most studies don't run blind comparisons
- **LLMs handle well:** Narrative text, demographics, goals, structured output, professional roles
- **LLMs handle poorly:** Diverse demographic representation (major rejection rates for transgender/non-binary), cross-cultural applicability (English bias), nuanced emotional depth, privacy-sensitive data
- **Token limitations** create problems for complex multi-thematic personas
- No standardized evaluation framework across the field

### "Generative AI Personas Considered Harmful?" (ScienceDirect, 2025)

- Expert survey (n=17): all 20 challenges rated as problematic (M > 4.0/7)
- Top concerns: hallucinations (M=5.94), over-sanitization (M=5.82), lack of standardization (M=5.59)
- 60% of challenges MORE problematic for GenAI than conventional personas
- Core recommendation: human-AI collaboration, not full automation

## Relevance to Unified Persona System + Knowledge Engine

**Validates:**
- LLM personas can reach expert-level acceptance when prompting is structured (one-shot/few-shot with domain context) in narrow professional domains
- Turing-test framing is legitimate evaluation framework adaptable for confidence scoring

**Does NOT validate (important caveats):**
- Brien's 178-entity registry contains named thought leaders (public figures), not target-user HCD personas. Different validity surface.
- "Indistinguishable" finding likely has translation artifact. LLM advantage may be language naturalism, not substantive quality.
- Consumer, healthcare, or multi-cultural contexts not covered.

**Actionable for persona-intake pipeline and confidence scoring:**
- **Origin tracking matters epistemologically**, not just for quality — tracks which claims are grounded in real human corpus vs. synthesized. Experts can't tell the difference in blind test, but that's surface acceptance, not epistemological grounding.
- **One-shot/few-shot with example personas** = validated generation approach. Aligns with providing rendered persona examples in persona-intake skill.
- **Language flow is most diagnostically discriminating signal** — worth tracking in origin metadata.
- **Evaluation gap:** Field has no standard rubric. Persona Perception Scale (authenticity, clarity, empathy, willingness to use) is most-cited structured instrument.
- **Confidence default:** Agent-generated personas should remain below human-curated absent specific validation. The 44% no-evaluation rate and 20 identified challenges support this.
