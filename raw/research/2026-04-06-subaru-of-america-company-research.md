## 3. Recent Strategic Moves (2024–2026)

### EV Strategy — Aggressive Catch-Up

Subaru entered the EV era late and knows it. The Solterra (launched 2022, co-developed with Toyota on e-TNGA platform) was widely viewed as rushed and underwhelming — mediocre range, not IRA-eligible initially, poor competitive positioning. But the 2025-2026 pivot is significant:

| Model | Type | Range | HP | MSRP | Status |
|-------|------|-------|----|------|--------|
| **2026 Solterra** | Compact EV SUV | 288 mi | 233/338 (XT) | ~$44,995 | On sale |
| **2026 Trailseeker** | Mid-size EV SUV | TBD | 375 | ~$49,995-$54,995 | Production started (Yajima plant, Japan) |
| **2026 Uncharted** | Subcompact EV CUV | 300+ mi | 338 (dual motor) | $34,995 | Announced; arriving at retailers |
| **TBD 3-row EV** | Large EV SUV | TBD | 420 | TBD | Global debut April 2026 NYIAS; built at Toyota Georgetown, KY |

All 2026 Subaru EVs are NACS-compatible (15,000+ charging locations). By end of 2026, Subaru plans four EV models — a massive expansion from one.

**Production expansion:** Subaru began in-house BEV production at Gunma Yajima Plant (Japan) — the E-Outback is the first. Previously all Subaru EVs were built at Toyota plants. Target: 200,000 EVs/year; combined 800,000 units/year by 2028 (Yajima + Oizumi doubled).

**Strategic read:** Subaru is running a parallel gas/electric strategy — maintaining its core ICE/hybrid lineup while rapidly scaling EVs. The Toyota partnership is the key enabler: shared platforms, shared manufacturing (Georgetown), shared R&D costs. Without Toyota, Subaru couldn't afford this transition at scale.

### Manufacturing & Tariff Exposure

**Tariff impact is severe.** Subaru faces a potential **$2.5B tariff hit** on US-bound vehicles and parts. In Q3 FY2025 (Oct-Dec 2025), Subaru reported an **operating loss of ¥36.4B (~$233M)**, directly attributing it to US tariff policy changes. Full-year tariff impact on operating profit: **¥229B negative** (¥44B worse than prior forecast).

**Response strategy:**
- Moving Forester production to Indiana (SIA) — started fall 2025; Forester Hybrid production began at SIA in early 2026
- Ending Legacy sedan production at SIA (spring 2026) and Outback production (end of 2026) to free capacity
- Exploring further domestic production increases and supplier capacity optimization
- The upcoming 3-row EV will be built at Toyota's Georgetown, KY plant — avoiding import tariffs

**Indiana plant (SIA):** Currently produces ~350,000 vehicles/year. Located in Lafayette, IN. Subaru has confirmed it is NOT leaving Indiana despite viral rumors. The plant is central to the tariff mitigation strategy.

### Retail Network Transformation — Connection Hub

Subaru unveiled the **"Connection Hub"** in September 2025 — its first full dealership redesign since 2007. This is a hospitality-driven retail experience transformation:
- Cafe-style lounges, immersive vehicle vignettes, digital customization tables
- Nature-inspired design with outdoor green spaces, dog parks, community event areas
- Designed by ChangeUp agency; scalable to all facility sizes
- Enrollment open now; retailers begin implementing in 2026

**Strategic read for engagement:** This is a significant signal that SOA is investing in experience transformation beyond just vehicles. The Connection Hub requires digital infrastructure, dealer management system integration, and change management — all adjacent to the IT/agile transformation Brien is advising on.

## 4. Technology & Digital Transformation

### Corporate DX Strategy (Subaru Corporation Level)

Subaru Corporation's DX strategy centers on two pillars:
1. **Monozukuri Innovation** — transitioning from department-siloed systems to cross-functional data environments; enabling real-time quality monitoring via tablet-based input at manufacturing sites
2. **Value Creation** — leveraging accumulated data for customer experiences

Key DX principles:
- **"Fit to Standard"** approach — adopting best-available technologies rather than custom-building
- **Agile development frameworks** for operation and maintenance
- **IT governance from "One SUBARU" perspective** — global standardization
- **Cybersecurity as central architectural concern**

### Subaru's Competitive Moats

1. **Symmetrical AWD on every model** — no competitor matches this. It's the brand's defining technical differentiator and drives disproportionate loyalty in snow belt / outdoor lifestyle markets.
2. **Consumer Reports #1 reliability (2025)** — rated 68/100, beating Lexus, Toyota, and Honda. Powerful marketing asset.
3. **ACSI top ranking (2025)** — highest customer satisfaction among mass-market brands.
4. **J.D. Power Sales Satisfaction** — ranked #2 among mass-market brands.
5. **Brand loyalty** — Subaru owners are famously devoted. The "Subaru Love" positioning resonates with a specific but passionate demographic (outdoor, progressive, dog-owning, Northeast/Pacific NW).

### Competitive Vulnerabilities

1. **Narrow product portfolio** — essentially a 3-model company (Crosstrek/Forester/Outback = ~80% of sales). Zero trucks. Limited sedan presence (Legacy discontinued). No luxury tier.
2. **EV latecomer** — Solterra sold ~14K units in 2025 vs. Tesla Model Y at ~750K. The gap is enormous.
3. **Tariff exposure** — $2.5B potential hit; more vulnerable than competitors with larger US manufacturing bases (Toyota, Honda have extensive US/Mexico production).
4. **Scale disadvantage** — at ~644K US sales, Subaru is 1/3.5 Toyota's volume. R&D costs per vehicle are significantly higher. The Toyota partnership partially offsets this.
5. **Hybrid gap** — Toyota's hybrid dominance is growing (RAV4 Hybrid is the segment benchmark). Subaru's hybrid offerings are newer and less proven.
6. **Regional concentration** — disproportionately dependent on Northeast and Pacific Northwest. Underrepresented in Sun Belt growth markets.

## 7. Industry Forces

### EV Transition Pressure

The industry is in a hybrid-to-EV transition, with automakers hedging between ICE, hybrid, and full EV strategies. Subaru's "parallel gas/electric" approach is pragmatic but expensive — it requires maintaining two complete powertrains and supply chains simultaneously. The Toyota partnership makes this financially viable but creates dependency.

**Policy uncertainty:** US EV incentive policy (IRA tax credits) and tariff policy are both unstable. Subaru's strategy of building EVs at Toyota's Kentucky plant (for IRA eligibility) and moving ICE production to Indiana (for tariff avoidance) shows sophisticated supply chain chess — but also vulnerability to policy shifts.

### Supply Chain

- **Tariffs are the dominant force** — $2.5B exposure, ¥229B operating profit hit
- Subaru's supply chain is more Japan-dependent than competitors, increasing tariff vulnerability
- Parts sourcing from Japan/Asia creates ongoing cost pressure even for Indiana-built vehicles
- The Toyota partnership provides manufacturing flexibility (Georgetown KY, shared supplier networks)

### Dealer Network Evolution

The Connection Hub redesign signals a fundamental rethink of automotive retail. Key dynamics:
- Shift from transactional dealership to experiential hospitality destination
- Digital + physical integration (customization tables, media walls, app-connected experiences)
- EV-specific infrastructure requirements (charging, service bay reconfiguration)
- The 2007-era facility standards are 19 years old — this redesign was overdue
