# The Ari Conversation — Primary Signal

> **Date:** 2026-03-28
> **Type:** Practitioner conversation
> **Significance:** Empirical validation of the Agile collapse pattern

---

## Context

Brien shared a conversation with engineer Ari about how AI is changing their team's development process. This became the empirical grounding for the entire Dev OS concept.

## Key Quotes

> "Agile scrum makes a lot less sense as a process when the implementation of anything takes hours at most."

> Tickets have become "specifications for bots to run"

> "Refinement meeting becomes HEAVILY design oriented"

- PRDs are developed by product *outside* Jira; epics operate at a broader scale than typical Jira tickets
- Brien pushed: "Where do you guys capture the problems you're solving for and why this is the approach you're taking?"

## Why This Matters

Ari's team discovered empirically what Cagan has theorized: **discovery was always the hard problem, not delivery.** AI just made the argument undeniable.

This conversation is the existence proof — a real team, in production, experiencing the shift that Dev OS is designed to address. Their tickets became specs. Their ceremonies became design sessions. Their process evolved before their methodology did.

## Connection to Dev OS

- Validates the **Ceremony Tax** (sprint ceremonies become overhead when implementation is hours)
- Validates the **Specification Gap** (tickets need to become real specifications)
- Validates the **cross-functional shaping** hypothesis (refinement becomes design-oriented)
- Surfaces the **observability question** (where do you capture the *why*?)
