Signals

Structured observability for work

Signals are the observable traces left by work. Every spec executed, every decision made, every experiment run—these leave signals. Signals make the notice→spec→execute→observe loop visible and improvable.

Signal Types

Execution Signals

Traces left when a spec is executed. Typically includes:

  • What was built
  • When it was built
  • Who built it (human or AI agent)
  • What the spec was
  • Artifacts (code, documents, outputs)
  • Time taken
  • Success or failure status

Observation Signals

Data collected when observing the results of execution. Examples:

  • Did it work as expected?
  • What surprised you?
  • What would you change?
  • Quantitative metrics (speed, quality, cost)
  • Qualitative feedback (easy to use, understandable, etc.)

Decision Signals

Signals from decisions made and their outcomes:

  • What decision was made
  • Why (reasoning)
  • When
  • Expected outcome
  • Actual outcome
  • Decisions that led to it (dependencies)

How Signals Are Used

Signals feed into the observe phase of the loop and then back into notice. As patterns emerge from signals:

  • You notice recurring problems and opportunities
  • You identify which specs produce the best outcomes
  • You understand which AI agents or tools work best for different tasks
  • You build feedback loops that improve over time
  • You share learnings with your team
