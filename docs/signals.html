Signal Stream

Observations captured from Intent's own development. Each signal has a confidence score, source, and related intents. This is what the observe layer looks like in practice.

Spec-driven dev is the natural successor to agile

Confidence: 0.92 · Source: Internal research · Related intents: Intent positioning

Teams who've tried AI-augmented workflows consistently report that ticket-based coordination breaks down. The friction isn't execution — it's spec quality. This suggests a category shift, not an optimization within agile.

Open development builds trust in early-stage tools

Confidence: 0.87 · Source: User feedback · Related intents: GTM strategy

Practitioners evaluating Intent respond positively to seeing decisions, tradeoffs, and open risks documented publicly. Transparency around what's not yet built matters more than feature completeness.

Observability is a forcing function for good architecture

Confidence: 0.79 · Source: Implementation experience · Related intents: Tech architecture

The requirement to emit structured events from every state transition naturally surfaces design clarity issues. Systems that can't be observed are systems that are poorly understood.

The gap is bigger in larger teams

Confidence: 0.85 · Source: Customer interviews · Related intents: Audience targeting

Solo practitioners and small teams can collapse workflow locally. The pain multiplies in orgs where process coordination is the primary tax. Intent's value increases with org size.

Three disciplines feel the shift differently

Confidence: 0.88 · Source: Persona research · Related intents: Positioning, feature prioritization

Architects see efficiency gains. PMs see validation of discovery-hard thesis. Designers see spec quality mattering. These three pathways converge on Intent but approach it differently.
