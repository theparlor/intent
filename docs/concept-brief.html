Intent — Product Concept Brief

Stage: Idea → ready for validation
Date: 2026-03-28
Author: Brien

The Problem

When AI collapses implementation time from weeks to hours, the bottleneck moves upstream — and the entire team operating model breaks.

Small product teams (3-5 people spanning product, engineering, design, and quality) adopted Agile to solve a coordination problem: how do you synchronize humans doing slow, uncertain work? But AI agents have detonated the assumption those ceremonies rest on: that delivery is the hard part.

This creates a cascade of failures:

The Ceremony Tax. Two-week sprint cycles impose artificial boundaries on work that now flows in hours.
The Specification Gap. Jira tickets were "enough to start a conversation." AI agents can't have hallway conversations. They need Intent (why), Shape (what good looks like), and Contract (boundaries).
The Silo Persistence. Sequential handoffs (product → design → engineering → QA) are absurd when agents don't respect role boundaries.
The Observability Void. Agent reasoning trapped in prompt windows that close and vanish.

Root Cause

The industry bolts AI onto the existing Agile model rather than questioning whether the model itself still fits. Motor on a horse-drawn carriage.

The Audience

Primary: Small Cross-Functional Product Teams (3-5 people)

Teams where product, engineering, design, and quality work closely together. They've already felt the pull of AI changing their tempo. They value flow over ceremony, want collective ownership without silos, and demand specs that connect the why to the work so any participant (human or agent) can decide locally.

Personas

The Practitioner-Architect — Senior IC who's collapsed their own workflow with AI but can't get the team's process to match.

The Product-Minded Leader — PM who reads Cagan, knows discovery was always the hard problem, sees AI as vindication.

The Design-Quality Advocate — Designer/QA frustrated that AI output is evaluated against ticket-level specs rather than UX-level intent.

The Solution: Intent

A team operating model where AI agents are first-class participants.

The Core Loop

NOTICE → SPEC → EXECUTE → OBSERVE → NOTICE

No sprint boundaries. Continuous flow. Team energy follows the highest-leverage work.

What Makes This Different

The spec-driven development movement (Spec Kit, Kiro, Tessl) solves the engineering execution layer. Intent operates one level up — the team operating layer.

Layer What It Solves Examples
Team Operating Model (Intent) How the team flows, decides, and learns This concept
Spec-Driven Development How specs become code via AI agents GitHub Spec Kit, Kiro, Tessl
AI Coding Assistants How individuals get AI help Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor

Intent sits at the intersection of product thinking (Cagan, Torres, Patton), spec-driven development (Spec Kit, Kiro), and agent observability (Entire.io).

Differentiation

vs AI-Augmented Agile: They preserve ceremonies and add AI within them. Intent argues the ceremonies are the wrong container.

vs Spec-Driven Dev Tools: Excellent at the engineering layer. Don't address how specs get shaped (upstream) or how teams learn from outcomes (downstream).

vs Cagan's Product Model: Philosophically aligned. But designed for weeks-long delivery. Intent is the AI-native implementation.

Hypotheses

H1: The Problem Is Real
Teams using AI agents experience ceremony/process mismatch.
Test: 20 teams self-identify within 60 days of manifesto.
H2: Specification Gap Is Core Pain
The frustration is "we can't specify well enough."
Test: Top-3 pain point in 10 team interviews.
H3: Cross-Functional Shaping Is the Unlock
Involving product/design/quality in specs produces better outcomes.
Test: Compare cross-functional vs engineering-only spec shaping.
H4: Methodology Before Tooling
Teams adopt the loop with existing tools first.
Test: 5 teams sustain Intent loop 3+ months with markdown + Git.
H5: Thought Leadership Creates Demand
Manifesto generates consulting inbound.
Test: 3+ inquiries within 90 days.

Risks

"Just write better tickets" dismissal Manifesto must articulate that the loop is the innovation, not just the spec
Agile incumbency Frame as "post-Agile for AI teams," not "anti-Agile"
Premature tooling Staged approach mitigates, but temptation will be strong
Team size boundary Does this scale beyond 5? Initial focus is deliberate
Enterprise adoption path Team within a machine problem
