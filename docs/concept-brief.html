Concept Brief

Intent as a product: the problem, audience, solution, differentiation, go-to-market hypotheses, and open risks. For anyone evaluating the venture angle.

The Problem

Agile ceremonies were designed for coordinating human labor. When AI agents do the building, the old machinery breaks down. Standup, sprints, and story points become friction. The real bottleneck shifts upstream: the quality of what you ask for matters more than the process you follow.

The Audience

Three personas feel this acutely:

  • Senior ICs who've collapsed their own workflow with AI but watch their teams stuck in agile theater.
  • PMs who see AI as vindication of the hard problem (discovery, not execution) and want the org to match.
  • Designers and QA frustrated that AI output is evaluated against ticket-level specs instead of UX intent.

The Solution

Intent replaces ceremonies with a continuous notice-spec-execute-observe loop. Work units are declarative contracts agents execute against, not prose tickets humans update. State lives in git, changes emit structured events, and observability is ambient.

Differentiation

This isn't agile optimization. It's a category shift: from human-labor coordination to agent-augmented teams. The mental model is spec-driven development, not ticket-driven project management.

Open Risks

Adoption requires unlearning agile habits. Teams accustomed to sprints and standups must shift to continuous flow and signal-driven intake. Tooling is early.
