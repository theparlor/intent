# Persona: Transformation Consultant

## Who

Brien Tate — the embedded transformation advisory consultant on the Turnberry team, serving as the product operating model and strategic alignment specialist for the Subaru M.A.R.S. engagement. 30 years of progressive experience: IC to management to executive to consulting/coaching. Six-domain practice: product strategy, product management, engineering leadership, agile transformation, organizational design, and executive advisory.

This is a **role persona** — Brien operating within the specific constraints of the Subaru engagement. The role is shaped by three structural realities:

1. **Intermediary dynamics.** Brien works for Subaru through Turnberry Solutions. He has no direct client contract. Turnberry controls scope, billing, and engagement structure. Brien's advisory relationship with Subaru is strong (the client trusts him and specifically requested his return), but his commercial relationship runs through an intermediary that pays 55% below market rate.

2. **Recovery context.** Brien was brought back after Turnberry's initial FTE placement failed in week 1. This means the client has heightened expectations ("we got the good one this time") and Turnberry has heightened accountability ("don't let this one fail too").

3. **Multi-domain mandate.** The advisory function owns product strategy and operating model advisory, but influences coaching approach, engagement sequencing, expansion strategy, and team-level recommendations. The "owned vs. influenced" boundary is deliberately permeable — Brien operates in the influence zone as much as the owned zone.

## Behaviors

- Builds artifacts that outlast the engagement. The org chart (157 staff mapped), reconciled schedule, curriculum view, decision log, internal overlay — these are designed to be transferable, not consultant-dependent. If Brien leaves, the work product remains navigable.
- Operates at multiple altitude levels within the same week. Monday Agile Office Hours (team-level coaching), platform leader sessions (mid-level advisory), bi-weekly status (executive reporting), internal overlay (strategic observation). Switches context rapidly.
- Documents everything the client doesn't see. The Internal Overlay (sensitive notes, risk signals, political context) is the "real" engagement record. Client-facing deliverables are polished; internal artifacts are candid.
- Navigates the Turnberry team dynamic carefully. Jason is the engagement lead (controls scope and status). Dean is the agile lead coach (primary delivery seat). Omar handles JIRA. Brien holds the advisory seat — a role that is structurally ambiguous because it touches everything but owns little explicitly.
- Pushes for scope corrections proactively. The SOW-to-reality reconciliation (Kanban cancelled, R&R added, team names corrected, week numbering aligned) was Brien-initiated. He treats the SOW as a living contract, not a fixed document.
- Uses design thinking and visual artifacts to communicate with non-technical stakeholders. HTML deliverables, interactive Gantt charts, collapsible org visualizations — the medium IS the message for Brien's advisory style.

## Navigating the Intermediary

The Turnberry relationship creates specific constraints:

- **Rate:** $125/hr contracted vs. $275/hr market rate (55% gap). Effective rate after 1099 burden: $66.28/hr fully loaded. This creates a constant economic tension — Brien is delivering disproportionate value relative to compensation.
- **Scope control:** Jason (Turnberry Engagement Lead) controls scope. Brien can recommend but not unilaterally add or remove deliverables.
- **Client trust asymmetry:** Subaru trusts Brien more than they trust Turnberry generically. This is leverage for Brien personally but risk for Turnberry — they need Brien to succeed but don't want him to become indispensable in a way that bypasses their engagement structure.
- **Staffing decisions:** Omar was removed from the engagement and then re-added. Brien has no control over Turnberry's staffing decisions but absorbs their consequences.

## Needs & Pain Points

- PP-001: Advisory mandate is broad (product, engineering, agile) but authority is narrow — influence without control, mediated through Turnberry's engagement structure
