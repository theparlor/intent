# Context Dossier: Subaru M.A.R.S. Agile Transformation Engagement

## Scope

20-week Agile Transformation engagement targeting Subaru of America's M.A.R.S. (Marketing Automation Retail Solutions) and Retail Systems group. Runs **February 23 -- July 17, 2026**. Delivered through Turnberry Solutions. 99% onsite at SOA headquarters in Camden, NJ (Mon/Tue/Thu onsite, Wed remote from hotel, Fri from home).

The engagement starts with M.A.R.S. Platforms as a proving ground under VP Greg Giuffrida, with a clear expansion path to other IT groups (ERP, Data & Analytics / CRM, Infrastructure & Ops). SOA's full IT organization is approximately 120 people, split roughly 50/50 employees and contractors.

**In scope:** 6 teams within M.A.R.S. and Retail Systems -- training delivery, embedded coaching, JIRA standardization and baselining.

**Out of scope:** Areas outside M.A.R.S. Platforms, contractors (for training purposes), engineering/DevOps coaching, custom JIRA integrations and reporting.

## Three Focus Areas

### 1. Training & Toolkit (Agile Academy)
Structured curriculum delivered to in-scope teams. **16 courses across 4 categories** (15 active after Kanban was cancelled at client request, replaced by Roles & Responsibilities).

| Category | Courses |
|----------|---------|
| **Foundations** | Agile Mindset, Product Mindset, Navigating Change |
| **Customer Centricity** | Challenging Assumptions, Aligning on Value (Story Maps & MVP), Customer Outcomes (Leading/Lagging Indicators) |
| **Delivery** | Achieving Flow, Estimation, Definition of Done/Ready, User Stories/Tasks/BL Fundamentals, Backlog Item Writing, Backlog Item Splitting Deep Dive, Adaptability |
| **Frameworks** | Scrum Fundamentals, Roles & Responsibilities, Psych Safety |

Format types: Coaching (C), Leadership Team first (LT), Formal Training (F), Team Session (T). Many courses follow LT-then-F sequencing.

### 2. Coaching
Embedded coaching supporting adoption through:
- Platform leader coaching sessions (fortnightly leadership connects)
- Team-level coaching on events, artifacts, and behaviors
- Office Hours -- Monday in-person (3-4 PM) and Wednesday online (12-1 PM)
- Product strategy framework workshops (vision, outcomes, ecosystem mapping)

### 3. Baselining JIRA Tool Usage & Metrics
Led by Omar Rashid. Four-phase approach:
1. Getting Started -- access, initial assessment
2. Capture Current State -- document per-team JIRA usage
3. Draft Future State -- design standardized configuration
4. Migration -- execute transition

Target outcomes: consistent reporting on standard metrics, consistent behavior across teams, Scrum or Kanban board choice, multi-audience dashboards.

## Constraints

1. **Turnberry intermediary.** Brien is a 1099 contractor to Turnberry, not to Subaru directly. All engagement decisions flow through Jason Lindmeyer (Turnberry Engagement Lead). Brien has influence but not control over scope and sequencing.

2. **Rate: $125/hr.** 55% below Brien's market rate of $275/hr (E7 equivalency). Effective rate after 1099 burden: ~$66.28/hr fully-loaded. Fixed bid -- Turnberry absorbs overrun risk.

3. **40-hour weekly cap.** No overtime. Brien must maximize leverage within a fixed time box each week.

4. **1099, zero benefits.** No health insurance, PTO, 401k, or other employment benefits. Self-employment tax burden adds ~$19k on $90k gross.

5. **99% onsite, 3 days/week.** BOS-to-PHL travel required. T&E fully reimbursed by Turnberry.

6. **Recovery staffing context.** Brien was brought back after Turnberry's FTE replacement failed in week 1. Client (Greg/Gino) trusts Brien specifically. This creates personal leverage but also personal dependency.

7. **Vocabulary standards.** Modern agile language enforced: "events" not "ceremonies," "refinement" not "grooming," "Scrum Lead" not "Scrum Master."

8. **POs are tech POs.** Business partners not heavily involved initially. Product Owner function starts technical, not cross-functional.

## Assumptions

Each assumption is testable. If proven wrong, it changes engagement strategy.

1. **Leadership buy-in will hold.** Greg and Gino are sponsors, but "leadership holding us back" was a noted discovery finding. If mid-level leadership resists, training adoption stalls. *Test: Observe leadership attendance and behavior in fortnightly connects and training sessions.*

2. **M.A.R.S. is representative enough to be a proving ground.** The expansion path assumes M.A.R.S. success translates to other IT groups (ERP, CRM, Infra). If M.A.R.S. is too different from the rest, the playbook won't transfer. *Test: Compare M.A.R.S. team structures and pain points to ERP/CRM teams during engagement.*

3. **Omar's JIRA workstream will stabilize.** Omar was removed and re-added to the engagement. If JIRA standardization slips, the metrics and dashboarding pillar collapses -- and with it, the ability to show measurable outcomes. *Test: Track Omar's delivery against the 4-phase plan week over week.*

4. **Individual contributor enthusiasm survives leadership friction.** ICs were excited in discovery. If the transformation creates more process overhead before showing benefit, enthusiasm erodes. *Test: Monitor sentiment in Office Hours sessions and team coaching.*

5. **20 weeks is enough to reach PI Planning #2.** The timeline assumes consistent progress. Snow delays already pushed Week 0. If training delivery slips, the midpoint and close milestones compress. *Test: Track schedule adherence weekly via the Gantt/planogram.*

6. **Kanban cancellation was the right call.** Replaced by R&R. If teams need flow-based work management (especially QA, DevOps), the gap will surface as a coaching problem. *Test: Observe whether teams default to Kanban-like behaviors without formal training.*

7. **Brien's advisory influence exceeds his formal authority.** Brien owns advisory but influences scope, coaching, and expansion strategy. If Jason or Turnberry constrain that influence, Brien's impact shrinks to training design only. *Test: Track whether Brien's recommendations are acted on or sidelined.*

8. **Tech POs can evolve into cross-functional POs.** Starting with tech POs is pragmatic but limits product thinking. If business partners never engage, the Product Mindset training has no soil. *Test: Observe whether any business stakeholder engagement emerges by Week 10.*

## Strategic Questions

These drive the research agenda and shape Brien's advisory focus.

1. **What does "success" look like at Week 20 that Greg can take to the CIO?** The expansion path depends on Greg having a compelling story. What metrics, behaviors, and artifacts will Laura Matey find convincing?

2. **How do we measure transformation progress without gaming metrics?** Team Scorecards (velocity, predictability, cycle time) can be gamed. What leading indicators are more honest -- and can they be extracted from JIRA?

3. **What is the right expansion sequencing after M.A.R.S.?** CRM (8 people, MS Dynamics) is identified as a target. But is it the highest-leverage next step, or should the engagement target a group with more pain?

4. **Where does Brien's advisory function end and Dean's coaching function begin?** Dean holds both coaching and advisory responsibilities. Brien brings product operating model expertise. The boundary is fuzzy. If it stays fuzzy, either duplicate effort occurs or gaps open.

5. **Can this engagement produce reusable IP for Brien's consulting practice?** Frameworks, assessment tools, curriculum design -- what from this engagement can be abstracted into Core/ without violating client confidentiality?

6. **What is the rate renegotiation trigger?** Brien is at $125/hr, 55% below market. The recovery staffing context gives leverage. When and how does this conversation happen -- and what outcome is realistic?

7. **How does the JIRA standardization connect to the coaching narrative?** If JIRA becomes a compliance exercise ("everyone use the same fields") rather than a behavior-change enabler, it undermines the transformation thesis. How do we connect tooling standardization to the coaching outcomes?

8. **What happens when the engagement ends?** Week 20 close means the Turnberry team leaves. Who sustains the practices? Is there an internal champion strategy, a CoP plan, or a follow-on engagement structure?
