## §1 Problem statement

The v1 gate at `Core/frameworks/methodology-library/meta/signal-scoring.md` is a weighted sum of five facets:

```
trust = clarity·0.30 + (1−blast_radius)·0.20 + reversibility·0.20 + testability·0.20 + precedent·0.10
```

Weight audit:
- ~0.90 of the formula measures downside-of-failure (clarity, inverted blast radius, reversibility, testability).
- ~0.10 measures feasibility (precedent).
- 0.00 measures strategic value or upside.

The formula is monotonically restrictive by construction. A panelist cannot vote bold inside this rubric; the only available move is to decline to lower a factor. The persistent persona-panel lock-down behavior observed across Intent-enabled products is the structurally-expected output of this math, not an RLHF artifact alone.

A gate with no value term operationalizes caution, not strategy. Intent's stated purpose is to operationalize *strategy*. v1 misses its own brief.

## §2 The aeronautical reframe

Flight is not additive. Lift is generated by thrust over a wing; induced drag rises as you generate more lift; gravity is the constant you fight. You cannot solve for one force without solving the others simultaneously. The autonomy gate is the same shape.

| Force | Symbol | Composition |
|---|---|---|
| **Gravity** (always-pulling-down stakes) | W | blast_radius × exposure × irreversibility |
| **Thrust** (engine) | T | strategic_value × λ |
| **Lift** (what holds you aloft against W) | L | containment_posture + detection_speed + recovery_options |
| **Drag** (caution overhead) | D | approval_latency + stage_gate_friction + re-explanation_tax + retraining_cost |

**λ (coefficient of bravery)** is the throttle. It is a tunable dashboard parameter, scoped per-product (theparlor solo repos = high λ; cross-human-comms work = low λ). It is NOT a personality trait of any model. It lives in `.intent/INTENT.md` per product, and is fit from each product's autonomy-drift signal corpus.

## §3 The two crash modes

The v1 weighted-sum gate models exactly one crash: overspeed (too much T, not enough L → structural failure). It does not model stall.

| Crash | Mechanism | Symptom in Intent today |
|---|---|---|
| **Overspeed** | Agent acts past its recovery capacity. L < W under current T. | Rare. The gate over-protects against this. |
| **Stall** | Agent acts too cautiously. T → 0, L decays for lack of practice, Observe loop receives no signal, calibration corpus stops growing, capacity to act safely literally bleeds off. | Pervasive. Proposal-framing on L4-eligible reversible work. Persona-panel lock-down. The autonomy-drift-pattern memory has documented this as a recurring behavior across products. |

Stall is the worse crash for Intent specifically, because Intent's safety property is the *loop* (Notice → Spec → Execute → Observe). A gate too tight starves Observe of data. The instrument calibration decays. The gate gets even tighter the next cycle.

## §4 The architectural unlock — estimate-decide split

The LLM is catastrophic at "what autonomy level?" because it regresses toward the do-no-harm population average. The LLM is genuinely good at "what's the blast radius here, roughly, and how confident am I?" — local estimation under uncertainty.

The decision flow inverts accordingly:

```
LLM:   {strategic_value, blast_radius, irreversibility, containment_posture,
        detection_speed, recovery_options, exposure}  with per-input uncertainty
                                ↓
Deterministic coupled model (this spec):
        W = blast_radius × exposure × irreversibility
        T = strategic_value × λ
        L = containment_posture + detection_speed + recovery_options
        D = approval_latency + stage_gate_friction + re-explanation_tax
        airworthy ⇔ L ≥ W  AND  T > stall_threshold(L, D)
                                ↓
Autonomy band  +  envelope position  +  recommended action
```

λ tuning is a knob a human operator sets, not an LLM personality dial. RLHF risk-aversion is bypassed.

## §5 Containment is engineerable — Lift is manufactured

v1 treats blast radius as a fixed property of the task. It is not. It is engineerable. Sandbox, branch, shadow deploy, canary, transaction-with-rollback, dry-run mode, feature flag, ephemeral wiki, snapshot — every one of these is a **Lift investment** that buys autonomy. The flight model makes this an explicit economic decision: spend on Lift to fund Thrust.

This is the operational consequence of the [AI lowers cost of overbuilding](memory/feedback_overbuilding_calculus.md) feedback rule. Containment is now cheap to manufacture; the calculus that produced v1's conservative bias was priced before that became true.

Reversibility is therefore not a score to assess; it is a budget to spend.

## §6 V1 speed — rollback vs fail-forward is envelope-dependent

A pilot rejects takeoff before V1 (decision speed). After V1, the airplane is committed — the safe move is to fly through the failure and divert. The recovery primitive is a function of where you are in flight, not a fixed policy.

For Intent operators:
- Before commit: rollback is the recovery primitive. Cheap and complete.
- After commit (artifacts shipped, downstream consumers in-flight): fail-forward is the recovery primitive. Forward-fix and divert; do not reflexively reach for `git revert`.

The fluidity of this distinction is what the v1 gate's "reversibility" facet collapses into a single number. v2 separates the two.

## §7 Cockpit vs Tower — altitude-differentiated decision rights

| Role | Tempo | Scope | Mapped to |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cockpit** | Fast, tactical, local | Engineer + agent in-session | Topography (scoring/active/handoff) |
| **Tower** | Slow, strategic, portfolio | Cross-session, multi-product | Loom (7-verb cross-session port) |

Autopilot (the deterministic coupled model from §4) flies the continuous math all day. The Cockpit reserves the handful of decisions that will never be programmable — the novel ethical call, the ditch-in-the-river decision. The Tower owns portfolio-altitude shifts in λ, capacity reallocation, and strategic constraints.

## §8 Calibrating λ from the drift corpus

λ is fit, not guessed. The fit data is the autonomy-drift signal corpus already accumulating in `.intent/signals/SIG-*.md` and `.intent/decisions.md` across products. Each signal carries (inputs, outcome, was-the-grant-right). The fit objective minimizes both crash modes simultaneously:

```
loss(λ) = Σ overspeed_cost(λ; signal_i) + Σ stall_cost(λ; signal_i)
```

The hundreds of drift signals Brien treated as "the gate is wrong" are precisely the labeled training set for "what λ would have been right." Discovery-first inventory of the corpus is the prerequisite step (see ratification_dependencies).

The other Claude's inventory script (`intent_signal_inventory.py` in the source transcript) is a starting point but its field-name assumptions (`boundary_crossed`, `trust_factors`, `human_decision`) do not match Brien's actual schema. A re-grounded inventory pass is the first build step after this spec is reviewed.

## §9 Shadow-autonomy protocol — find the ledge by measurement

The principled way to find the flight envelope is not to raise the harm ceiling; it is to instrument controlled probes. Generalized from the OVS drift-monitor primitive (post-hoc-first → live-after-threshold):

1. Agent operates one autonomy level *hotter* than currently granted, in dry-run/proposal-capture mode.
2. Witness logs what the agent would have done, with full input vector.
3. Diff against the human-approved decision when it lands.
4. After N runs, the agreement rate at L+1 is measured. If agreement crosses threshold, the live grant promotes.

This is calibration-as-flight-test. It replaces "is this safe?" guesswork with measured envelope mapping. It depends on Witness being a mandatory recorder (see §11 dependency).

## §10 Variance preservation — Voices' conservation law applies

The §4 estimate-decide split needs an uncertainty input. The wrong move is to ask one LLM "how sure are you?" — that gets RLHF-flattened to confident-or-vague.

The right move is to ask a **panel** with diverse priors and read the variance across them. Voices' conservation law applies directly: contradictory persona estimates produce two events, never one merged estimate. The variance across panel members IS the uncertainty quantity the flight model needs.

The composition recipe is the Forge operator candidate `panel-critique-v2-balanced` (see §11 dependency):

## §12 Open questions for ratification review

- Is λ scalar or vector? (Per-facet bravery may be needed — e.g., bold on Thrust, cautious on Drag tolerance.)
- How does this compose with the existing 4-layer hook architecture? Hooks remain the deterministic floor (§14). Does the flight model output map directly to hook preconditions, or does an intermediate "envelope check" layer fit between?
- Is "fail-forward vs rollback" decided by the deterministic model or surfaced as a Cockpit choice? (Probably the latter for novel cases; deterministic for in-corpus patterns.)
- Detection latency is currently bundled into Lift. Does it warrant a fifth force? (Engineering intuition: keep it in Lift; argue if data shows otherwise during calibration.)

## §13 What this spec is NOT replacing

- **The 4-layer hook architecture.** Hooks are the deterministic floor (deterministic preconditions that must hold, regardless of trust score). The flight model is the model that *computes the score*. The hooks already operate at: Layer 1 (memory/feedback policy), Layer 2 (spec amendment), Layer 3 (skill/agent), Layer 4 (Stop hook on bare-choice-without-recommendation, closure-discipline literal-term checks), Layer 5 (PreToolUse dispatch-prompt checks).
- **The autonomy-grant-enforcement spec.** That spec describes the *enforcement mechanism* (hooks). This spec describes the *gate model* (what the hooks are enforcing). They are siblings.
- **The closure-discipline spec.** Closure DoD remains: resolved requires `upstream_control_path:` and `catch_mechanism:`. The flight model adds a value-funded autonomy band, not a relaxation of closure standards.

## §14 The frame correction

Intent is not the safety layer; Intent is the reference implementation of *Coherence Engineering* (`Core/frameworks/coherence-engineering/DEFINITION.md`). The autonomy gate is one mechanism in the discipline. Reframing as "operationalize coherence" rather than "operationalize strategy as risk-gated execution" removes the structural bias toward caution at the framing layer, not just at the formula.

Below Intent's altitude live the prompt-shaping shopping-list ecosystems (SuperClaude, the Claude Code plugin lists). They cannot run a flight model — they have no substrate for L (no Witness), no variance source (no Voices), no V1 distinction (no commit anchor), no Tower (no Loom). The flight model is defensible precisely because it is above where the popular discourse plays.

## §16 λ-scoping convention (ratified 2026-05-26)

λ is **not a system constant**. It is persisted per-product as a top-level field in each product's `.intent/INTENT.md`, refit per-product from that product's drift-signal corpus. Products may optionally override λ per surface.

### Schema

```yaml
lambda_settings:
  default: 1.0           # baseline coefficient of bravery for this product
  per_surface:           # optional overrides keyed by gate-surface-matrix row
    repo_git_commit: 1.8 # high — theparlor solo repos
    repo_git_push: 1.8
    filesystem_write: 1.5
    cross_human_comms: 0.0  # L0 by autonomy grant — never raise
  rationale: |
    Per-actor scoping per WS-DDR-098 (Witness mandatory-recorder, ratified 2026-05-26)
    and feedback_commit_autonomy memory. Solo blast-radius surfaces
    get high λ; cross-human-notification surfaces stay at 0.
  last_fit: 2026-05-26
  fit_source: Core/frameworks/intent/tools/intent_signal_inventory.py
```

### Defaults by actor topology

| Actor topology | λ default | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Solo Brien-owned (theparlor/*, Workspaces-local) | 1.5–2.0 | feedback_commit_autonomy + L4 grant on file creation |
| Engagement-scoped consulting (Work/Consulting/*) | 1.0 | Standard practitioner cadence |
| Cross-human comms (Slack/email/calendar/PRs-with-reviewers) | 0.0 | L0 by autonomy grant — locked floor |
| Strategic / irreversible (data migrations, money, supply chain) | 0.0–0.3 | Closure-discipline + WS-DDR ratification gate |

### λ refit triggers

A product's λ is refit when ANY of the following holds:
- Drift-signal volume crosses 50 new signals since last fit
- Closure rate (resolved / (resolved + symptom-repaired)) crosses ±10% since last fit
- An overspeed incident is recorded (signal type: `autonomy-overspeed`)
- The actor topology changes (e.g., engagement promotes from advisory to consulting)

Refit invokes `intent_signal_inventory.py` followed by the (future) `lambda_fit.py` tool. Until `lambda_fit.py` lands, refit is manual via the per-product closure-rate observed in inventory output.
