# Decision: Staged GTM — thought leadership → methodology → tooling (conditional)

> Ratified 2026-03-28. All 4 autonomy-grant gates pass.

## Context

Building tooling before validating the methodology is a common failure mode. Brien is a solo practitioner with finite capacity. The highest-leverage initial move is content and interviews — not engineering.

## Decision

Stage the GTM: (1) thought leadership — manifesto + case studies; (2) methodology product — playbook + workshops; (3) tooling — conditional on practitioner validation. Tooling phase requires 5 in-depth interviews with teams experiencing AI + Agile friction before committing to tooling investment.

## Scope

Governs overall GTM sequence. Complements DEC-INTENT-002 (methodology first). Does not constrain building internal tooling for Brien's own use during phase 1/2.

## Alternatives Not Taken

| Alternative | Why not chosen | Reversibility if revisited |
|-------------|----------------|---------------------------|
| Tooling-led GTM | Premature; risk of building wrong tool | L2 — sunk cost |
| Consulting-led GTM | Scales poorly; delays productization | L3 |
| Simultaneous all-phase launch | Dilutes focus; depletes solo-practitioner capacity | L3 |

## Reversibility

L3 — GTM timeline may be optimistic for solo practitioner. If interviews reveal faster-than-expected demand for tooling, phase can be accelerated. Sunk thought-leadership content is still valuable.

## Ratification Action

Three-phase roadmap established. 5-interview gate is the unlock for tooling investment. Quality flag: GTM timeline may be optimistic.
