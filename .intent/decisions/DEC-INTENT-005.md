# Decision: Target practitioner-architects first

> Ratified 2026-03-28 (refined 2026-04-09). All 4 autonomy-grant gates pass.

## Context

Intent had to choose a primary persona for initial validation and GTM. Practitioner-architects — senior ICs on AI-augmented teams — feel the gap most acutely and have the org influence to adopt and advocate.

## Decision

Target practitioner-architects (senior ICs, system thinkers) as the primary user for initial GTM and discovery. Refined by DEC-20260409-02: staff+ engineers on teams of 2–7 using Claude Code daily; sweet spot teams of 2–5.

## Scope

Governs discovery interview recruitment, hero copy, site IA. Does not exclude other personas from the methodology — they are secondary users.

## Alternatives Not Taken

| Alternative | Why not chosen | Reversibility if revisited |
|-------------|----------------|---------------------------|
| Product managers first | Less likely to adopt file-native tooling; lower org influence for bottoms-up adoption | L3 |
| Engineering managers first | Ceremony burden is real but they have less personal pain with current tooling | L3 |
| All personas simultaneously | Dilutes positioning; "for everyone" = "for no one" (Dunford) | L3 |

## Reversibility

L3 — if discovery reveals a different natural primary user, reframe site and discovery protocol. 5 interviews are the validation gate.

## Ratification Action

Four-persona model defined (△ Practitioner-Architect primary). Recruitment filter for discovery interviews set to staff+ engineers on 2–7 person teams using Claude Code.
