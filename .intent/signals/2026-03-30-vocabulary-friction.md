# SIG-019: "Notice" and "Execute" aren't landing as loop phase names

## Observation

Brien flagged that two of the four loop phase names aren't resonating:

- **"Notice"** — feels too passive. The act is more like sensing, catching, or surfacing something worth paying attention to. It's active pattern recognition, not passive reception.
- **"Execute"** — feels too mechanical. What actually happens in this phase is creative building against a spec, with judgment and adaptation. "Execute" sounds like following orders.

The other two phases ("Spec" and "Observe") feel solid.

## Why This Matters

These are the bookends of the core loop — the entry point and the action point. If they don't feel right to the creator of the methodology, they won't feel right to practitioners. Naming is the user interface of a mental model.

## Candidate Replacements (To Workshop)

**For "Notice":**
- Sense — active sensing, pattern recognition
- Surface — something emerging into visibility
- Catch — more active, implies alertness
- Detect — technical but precise
- See — simplest, but maybe too simple

**For "Execute":**
- Build — creative, implies making
- Shape — connects to Cagan/Patton shaping language
- Make — simple, active
- Deliver — outcome-oriented
- Ship — startup-native but narrow

## Constraint

Whatever we choose needs to work in the loop cadence: `___→Spec→___→Observe`. It should feel like a natural rhythm when said aloud.

## Promotion Candidate

This is a naming decision, not a spec. Should be captured in decisions.md once resolved.
