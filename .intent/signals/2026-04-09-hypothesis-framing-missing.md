# Site presents Intent as shipped product; Brien's instinct is to frame as active hypothesis

Brien's explicit instinct during 2026-04-09 session review: *"we need a far more honest declaration of this is a hypothesis that may not be achievable and is an active work in progress. We should find a way to describe what we feel is good and solid but tablestakes versus still working through but evolutionary."*

The panels independently confirmed this from a different angle: 6 of 8 treated the site as if it were pitching a shipped product, and their critiques reflected that assumption. They couldn't explicitly recommend "reframe as hypothesis" because they believed that reframe would weaken positioning. But the honest reframe actually strengthens it — it turns architectural immaturity from a bug into a feature ("come pressure-test with us") and it respects the reader's intelligence.

## Why this matters

- Matches the actual maturity state (4 products, 3 conceptual, 1 operational)
- Invites collaboration instead of selling certainty
- Gives operational gaps a proper home (they're expected in a hypothesis, not a shipped product)
- Aligns with Rumelt's "Good Strategy" posture: diagnose honestly, name the bet, show the hypothesis chain

## Required outcome

Every piece of content distinguishes three maturity states explicitly:
1. **Tablestakes** — solid, proven, baseline expectation (e.g., "specs as contracts" is Shape Up/TDD baseline)
2. **Evolutionary** — our hypothesis we're testing (e.g., "trust-scored autonomy via weighted signal math")
3. **Open question** — unresolved, actively pressure-testing (e.g., "double-loop learning as architected mechanism")

Specifically call out: the autonomy-and-execution-of-trust-via-signal-intent-challenge-iterate pattern is our most ambitious evolutionary bet and should be labeled as such, not presented as settled.
