# Productize the Accessible Coherence Framing — Brand Revamp + Thinker-Type Formats + Co-Brand/License to Turnberry

## Signal

The plain-language translation layer (`translation/intent-in-plain-language.md`) opened a productization
path Brien wants to pursue: revamp the personal brand + design system, produce the material in **multiple
formats testable by thinker type**, and route it to market via a **co-branded, licensed** arrangement with
Turnberry rather than building/selling solo.

This is L1 (agent-assist; every real decision below is Brien's). Captured so it isn't lost, with the
decision surfaces made explicit.

## What was noticed

1. **One format won't land for everyone — design to thinker type.** Named test readers as archetypes:
   - **Markus** → rigor-seeker. Risk: reads the plain one-pager as *too thin / hand-wavey*. Needs the
     depth and the enforcement mechanism (this is exactly why DDR-009 exists) visible, or he discounts it.
   - **Pete Fleming** → visual processor. Needs **pictures/diagrams** to process the model at all;
     prose-only will not land.
   - **Greg** (leader archetype) → vision-buyer. The plain, outcome-level framing likely **excites** him.
   - Implication: the same core must ship in ≥3 registers — *rigor/depth*, *visual/diagrammatic*,
     *executive/vision* — and Brien should **test design+visualization vs. plain text per archetype**,
     not assume one wins.

2. **Brand + design system revamp** is a prerequisite: produce the material with real design, style, and
   visualizations — as testable artifacts, not decoration.

3. **GTM structure — the partner problem.** Self-built trainings are possible, but without a training +
   sales partner it likely "goes nowhere." Proposed compromise: **Brien authors, co-brands, and licenses
   to Turnberry to sell; Pete Fleming involved as facilitator/trainer.**

4. **Commercial posture.** A real **rate card**. Turnberry gets **no preferential pricing and no
   exclusivity.** What they get instead: access to a **turnkey concept they don't have today**, and a
   **fully hydrated version of what their AI Practice could look like.**

## Hypotheses

- **H1 (thinker-type fit):** Conversion improves when format matches cognitive style — depth for Markus,
  diagrams for Pete, vision for Greg. Testable directly with those three as first readers.
- **H2 (partner leverage):** Co-brand + license to Turnberry beats solo build (distribution + facilitation
  Brien lacks) and beats a plain reseller deal (Brien keeps the brand and the IP). Pete as facilitator is
  the delivery arm Brien doesn't want to staff.
- **H3 (terms):** Non-exclusive + no-preferential-pricing is viable *because* the value to Turnberry is
  category access ("an AI Practice they don't have") not a discount. Exclusivity would transfer Brien's
  leverage for nothing.

## Decision Surfaces (Brien's to make)

1. **Build path:** solo trainings vs. license-to-Turnberry vs. co-brand+license (leaning co-brand+license).
2. **Terms:** confirm non-exclusive + no preferential pricing; define the rate card; define what "turnkey
   concept + hydrated AI Practice" includes as deliverables.
3. **Pete's role:** facilitator/trainer scope, and whether he co-develops the visual register (H1) or only
   delivers.
4. **Format investment:** how much design-system spend before the thinker-type test — cheapest test first
   (rough diagram for Pete, depth appendix for Markus, vision one-pager for Greg) vs. full brand revamp.
5. **Sequencing:** test the three archetypes on the *current* one-pager + a rough visual before investing
   in the brand revamp, so the design system is built on evidence.
