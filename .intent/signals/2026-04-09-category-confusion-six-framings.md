# Six category framings across six pages — Dunford clarity score 3/10

Positioning panel counted six different category framings on the current site:
1. "The operating model for what comes next" (pitch)
2. "Operating System for AI-Augmented Teams" (concept-brief)
3. "A personal operating system" (concept-brief, same page)
4. "Transformation OS" (methodology)
5. "Four-step loop" (methodology)
6. "Product portfolio governed by Intent" (products)

Cold Visitor confirmed: took 10 minutes to construct a mental model the site should have provided in the hero.

## Why this matters

- Category is the #1 attribute Dunford's positioning framework asks you to nail
- A reader who can't state the category in 5 seconds cannot remember the product
- Five of eight panels flagged this independently (including the cold visitor)
- The current multi-framing approach is a hedge — trying to reach everyone reaches no one

## Required outcome

- Pick ONE category. Candidate: "Specification-driven operating loop for AI-native engineering teams."
- Delete every alternative framing from every page
- Put the one category in hero, meta description, footer, HTML title, every nav bar
- Make the category declaration unmissable
