# Methodology lineage unacknowledged — OODA, PDCA, Build-Measure-Learn, Torres

Product Strategy and Strategy panels both flagged: Notice→Spec→Execute→Observe is a rename of patterns that go back to Boyd (OODA, 1976), Deming (PDCA), Ries (Build-Measure-Learn), and Torres/Cagan (continuous discovery). The site nowhere acknowledges this prior art — doubly strange because Brien's own 178-persona library contains every ancestor.

Direct quote from Product Strategy panel (Cagan voice): *"Add a Lineage section: OODA, PDCA, Lean Startup, Shape Up, Continuous Discovery, Torres's OST, Gilad's GIST. Credit the ancestors — your own persona library already contains every master this framework descends from."*

## Why this matters

- Intent as *synthesis* is a stronger story than Intent as *invention*
- Unacknowledged lineage signals naivete to senior practitioners who will immediately recognize the pattern
- Brien's instinct (from this session's conversation): keep service names genericized for portability, but NAME the methodology ancestors for credibility — this is a novel synthesis neither panel proposed

## Required outcome

- New site page: Lineage / Intellectual Foundations
- Each phase of the loop linked to its methodological ancestors with named attribution
- Torres, Patton, Cagan, Seiden, Boyd, Deming, Ries all credited directly
- Cross-reference to persona library entries
- Lineage visible in hero copy even (e.g., "building on continuous discovery (Torres), story mapping (Patton), and OODA (Boyd)")

## Novel synthesis (Brien's insight, 2026-04-09 session)

The right move is NOT "genericize everything" or "name everything" — it's **named methodology, genericized service**. Keep the abstract service names (Notice, Spec, Execute, Observe) because they're portable. Name the methodology heroes (Torres, Patton, Cagan) because credibility travels with lineage. This combines the Positioning and Product panel recommendations in a way neither proposed.
