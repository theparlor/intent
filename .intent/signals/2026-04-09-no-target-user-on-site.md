# Site has no target user — 6 of 8 review panels flagged independently

Six of eight independent review panels (ARB, Product Strategy, Positioning, Discovery/UX, Org Design, Cold Visitor) each flagged that the site never names a target user. The concept-brief oscillates between "personal operating system," "team operating system," and "business operating system" within the same paragraph. Positioning found six different category framings across six pages.

Direct quote from Product Strategy panel (Wille voice): *"A real PM leadership tool would know its buyer inside 30 seconds. This site doesn't."*

Direct quote from Positioning panel (Dunford voice): *"If the prospect has to do the work, you've already lost."*

The cold visitor took 10 minutes to construct a mental model the site should have provided in the hero.

## Why this matters

- Gates most other findings. Without a named user, reader-as-hero fixes, category clarity, and narrative coherence are all impossible.
- Convergent finding from 6 independent panels is strong signal that this is the structural problem, not a content polish issue.
- The current 8-product portfolio compounds it — no user can hold all 8 in mind at once.

## Required outcome

One named user. One sentence. On the hero page. Every alternative framing deleted. Candidate:
"For practitioner-architects (senior ICs using Claude Code daily) whose AI tools outran their process, Intent is a spec-driven operating loop that replaces tickets with executable specs and observable outcomes."
