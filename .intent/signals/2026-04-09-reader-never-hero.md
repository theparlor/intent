# Reader is never the hero — StoryBrand violation across the site

Four panels independently noted that the site makes Intent the protagonist, not the reader. Every sentence is "Intent brings...", "Intent prescribes...", "Intent replaces..." — never "you will...", "your team...", "your work...".

Direct quote from Positioning panel (Miller voice): *"Current: 'Agile was right. Your toolchain isn't.' Replace with: 'Your AI writes code in an afternoon. Your team still plans it in a sprint. Intent closes that gap.' Place the reader, not the product, as the hero."*

## Why this matters

- StoryBrand research shows reader-as-hero copy converts 2-3x better than product-as-hero
- Cold visitor explicitly noted this as a "reference manual tone" problem
- Reinforces the "no target user" finding — if you can't name the user, you can't make them the hero

## Required outcome

- Every page hero rewritten with "you/your" in the first sentence
- Intent reframed as the guide, not the hero
- CTAs written as verbs the reader does, not things Intent does

- Clarity: 0.85 (4 panels noted it)

- Reversibility: 1.0
- Testability: 0.9 (count "Intent" vs "you" in hero copy)
- Precedent: 0.9 (StoryBrand, Donald Miller)
