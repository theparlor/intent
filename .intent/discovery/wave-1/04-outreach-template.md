# Recruitment Outreach Template — Discovery Wave 1

> Three templates for three tiers. Copy, personalize, send. Do NOT pitch Intent. Do NOT promise anything specific.

## Tier 1 — Warm contact (known, respected, worked together)

**Subject:** Asking for 45 minutes of your brain

---

Hey [Name],

I'm doing some research and you're on a short list of people I'd most trust to tell me if I'm wrong about something.

I've been watching how AI dev tools are changing the shape of engineering work. Specifically: when implementation gets dramatically faster, what happens upstream — to the way teams spec, coordinate, and review work? My gut says a lot, but I don't trust my gut yet. I want to hear it from people who are actually using these tools every day.

I'm running 10 structured conversations over the next two weeks. 45 minutes, remote, video on. I'm not pitching anything — in fact, I'm trying hard NOT to pitch because I want to hear about your real experience, not have you react to mine. I'll share the synthesis back with everyone I talk to.

Worth 45 minutes of your time in the next two weeks? If yes, any time that works is fine — I'll meet you where you are.

Thanks,
[Brien]

---

**Why this works:**
- Starts with respect (they're on a short list)
- Frames as research, not pitch
- Names the curiosity, not a conclusion
- Offers reciprocity (synthesis shared back)
- Makes the ask concrete and small (45 min, remote)
- Zero commitment beyond the interview

**What to avoid:**
- "I'm building something and would love your feedback" (they'll react to you, not their own experience)
- "Quick question" (nothing is quick; be honest)
- Attachments / links to a site (contaminates the data)

## Tier 2 — Network referral

**Subject:** [Mutual connection]'s suggestion

---

Hey [Name],

[Mutual connection] mentioned you as someone I should talk to. I'm doing some research on how senior engineers and PMs are actually using AI dev tools day-to-day — and what breaks in the surrounding process when implementation gets much faster.

It's not a pitch. I'm having structured 45-minute conversations with 10 people, and I'm deliberately NOT showing anyone what I'm building because I want to hear your unfiltered experience. I'll share the synthesis with everyone I talk to.

If you've got 45 minutes in the next two weeks, I'd be grateful. Happy to work around your schedule.

[Brien]

---

**Why this works:**
- Name-drops the warm referral (establishes legitimacy fast)
- Same "research, not pitch" framing
- Same reciprocity offer

## Tier 3 — Cold outreach

**Subject:** Researching how senior ICs actually use Claude Code

---

Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out cold — apologies — because you're exactly the kind of practitioner I want to hear from. I'm doing research on how senior engineers and PMs actually use Claude Code / Cursor / Copilot day-to-day, and what happens to the surrounding process (specs, coordination, review) when implementation gets dramatically faster.

I'm not selling anything. I'm running 10 structured 45-minute conversations with people who use these tools daily, and I'm deliberately not pitching or demoing anything because I want to hear your unfiltered experience. I'll share the synthesis with everyone who participates.

If it's not for you, totally understand — no hard feelings. If it is, 45 minutes, remote, I'll work around your schedule.

Thanks for considering it,
[Brien]
[one-line credibility: e.g., "30 years in product + engineering across ..."]

---

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges the cold ask without apologizing for it
- Specific profile ("senior ICs using Claude Code daily") — signals you're not spamming
- Honest about the purpose
- Clear opt-out
- Very short credibility statement (don't brag, just establish legitimacy)

## Follow-up message (3 days after initial, if no response)

**Subject:** re: [original subject]

---

Quick bump in case the first message got buried. If this isn't your thing or the timing's off, a one-line "not now" is perfect and I'll get out of your inbox.

If you're interested but don't have 45 minutes, even 20 would work.

[Brien]

## Scheduling confirmation message

**Subject:** Confirmed: [Day] at [Time]

---

Thanks [Name] — we're set for [day] at [time] [timezone].

Video link: [Zoom/Meet/etc.]

What to expect:
- 45 minutes, video on
- I'll ask you about your actual recent work and tooling experience
- There's no demo, no pitch, no slides — it's a conversation
- I'll be taking notes (and recording if you're OK with it) so I can quote you accurately
- Anything you want kept off the record, just say so — I'll respect it
- I'll share the synthesis across all 10 conversations with you when it's done

Any questions, just reply. See you [day].

[Brien]

## After-interview thank you

**Subject:** Thank you — and a question

---

[Name],

Thanks for the 45 minutes today. The [specific thing they mentioned] is exactly the kind of thing I wouldn't have found without talking to you — it's not in any of my own assumptions going in.

Two quick things:
1. I'll send you the synthesis across all 10 interviews once it's done (probably 2-3 weeks out). I'll also send your interview writeup for review before it's used in the synthesis — you'll get to correct anything I mis-quoted or paraphrased.
2. Who else should I be talking to? If there's someone in your network (or a community you're part of) who'd have strong opinions about this, I'd love a referral.

Grateful for your time.

[Brien]

---

**The referral ask is doing real work here** — Tier 2 participants come from these closes.
